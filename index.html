Personal Statement

With all there is to know and understand, my philosophy is that learning is a life-long process. I am a humanist at heart and have strived to maximize my effective learning potential. This adventure has taken me from the galleries of the Louvre in Paris to the narratology and philology of various languages, in-depth archival work and the origins of scientific discourse to pure mathematics and digital functionality. A multifaceted approach to knowledge has made me adaptable to diverse situations, versatile in the application of my skillsets. Learning as a form of personal growth has improved my communication and decision-making skills.

Areas of expertise: I have a certificate in cybersecurity and experience in programming and algorithmic analysis, as well as website and API design and configuration. I have worked in many countries, including the United States, France, Egypt, Morocco and Iraq. My language skills include near-native fluency in French and Arabic. I have worked in IDP camps in Iraq as a counselor and human rights violation documenter. In addition, I have authored two books in French, received various federal research grants and been the Scholar-in-Residence at the National Endowment for Humanities. Currently, I am a professor and work with undergraduate students teaching foreign languages, cultures, and international relations. I have worked in conflict resolution in the Middle East, documenting refugee testimonial, creating a living archive of indigenous knowledge in North Africa and post-colonial research.
