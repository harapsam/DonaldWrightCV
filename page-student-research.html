  • “Exploring the Writings of Saddam Hussein’s General Ala al-din Al-Khammas, his Views on Military Leadership of the Early Caliphate, and his arguments towards understanding an Arab Art of War: Going Beyond the US military Interviewing Saddam’s Generals and Addressing the Gap in Analyzing their Books and Papers.”
    2023-present
    Youssef Aboul-Enein
    Al-Khammas is unique among Saddam Hussein’s generals in his training, while the bulk were trained by the Soviet Union and in the Middle-East, al-Khammas was a 1957 Sandhurst cadet, 1958 graduate of the British Infantry School, the British School of Armor, and U.S. Army Armor Center, when Iraq transitioned from a monarchy to a Republic that same year.
    Al-Khammas’ is also unique by the generation of Iraqi officers he trained, who commanded Iraqi combat units in the Iran-Iraq War (1980-1988), Desert Storm (1990-1991), and Iraqi Freedom (2003-2021). Al-Khammas also advocated that the Arabs have their own unique art of war that is just as worthy of study as the western way of warfare or eastern way of warfare.
  • "Exhibition Scenographic Design: A Holistic Approach to Interactive Spaces (The Moroccan Experience)"
    2013-2016
    Zineb Bahji
    Generating spatial dramaturgy inside museums has also been said to create an identity crisis of these institutions and supplant their cultural dimension and educational role with mere entertainment. For art museums, this practice has mostly been criticised for issues of authorship and interpretation. It is seen as a means of distraction that not only alters the traditional role proper to this institution but also interferes in the process contemplation, evaluation, and interpretation of artworks.
    Successful examples of exhibition scenographic representation clearly show that museum Scenography is an indispensible part of exhibition making and that the importance of this practice resides in three key elements: it serves to represent the objects to be displayed in a creative, engaging, and interactive manner, helps establish a dialogue among the basic material items and enhance their communicative value, and it engages the audience in an interactive experience, stimulates, guides, and acknowledges their interpretive skills.
